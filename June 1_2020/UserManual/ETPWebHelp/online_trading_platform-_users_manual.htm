Getting Started

The online trading platform is the first of its kind launched by ECX enabling market players to participate directly in a trade they are licensed with wherever they are. Previously users are required to come to the exchange to trade using the outcry system which requires the physical presence of the market player disregard to how far his/her location is.

 

The online trading platform is envisioned to increase access to ECX services, build capacity of various stakeholder groups and increase efficiency. Apart from this it also helps to increase the exchange’s reputation in using better technologies.

 

The system as any other interactive system has two main components the front end where users interact with the system and the back end where the actual business logic is performed and stored. The System is deployed at the data center in headquarter of the exchange; Regional Trading Centers (RTC) are connected to it which are used to conduct the online transactions.

 

In order to provide the intended services the system have the following five primary modules that will need to be integrated to form the complete ECX Trading Platform: User Management Module, Trader Interface module, Trade Matching Engine module, Back Office Interface Module and Data Feed Module.

Market Watch

 

Market Watch or Default Market Screen is one of the major sections in the Online Trading system’s home page, which enables authorized users (traders) to view the best (buy/bid and/or sell/ask) market transaction details of the opened/pre-opened sessions.

1.      Market Watch Data Grid Results:

a.      The system sorts the transactions (by TIME priority, i.e. First Entered Order on top) and display the Market Watch Lists Data Detail under the Market Watch Data Grid Result Section or displays Empty List with "No Market Data to Display" message if there is no transaction.

 

b.    The system displays the Best Sell/Ask Data Detail (AskQuantity and AskPrice) under the Market Watch Data Grid Result-Sell/Ask Section or displays Empty List with "No Market Data to Display" message if there is no transaction.

 

      • Best Sell/AskPrice => A Sell Pending Order with the Lowest Price

      • Ask Quantity =>could be a total/an aggregate amount of all Ask/Sell quantities with the best AskPrice.

 

c.       The system displays the Best Buy/Bid Data Detail (BidQuantity and BidPrice) under the Market Watch Data Grid Result-Bid/Buy Section or displays Empty List with "No Market Data to Display" message if there is no transaction.

 

      • Best Buy/BidPrice => A Buy Pending Order with the Highest Price

      • Bid Quantity=> could be a total/an aggregate amount of all  Buy/Bid   quantities with the best BidPrice.

 

d.      The system checks and displays the Best Price information (Last, High, Low, Open, Change, Vol) under the Market Watch Data Grid Section or should display Empty List with "No Market Data to Display" message if there is no transaction.

      • Last=>Price of Lastly Executed & Accepted Trade (per contract which is a combinations of Grade + Warehouse + Production Year),

      • High=>The Highest Price of among Executed & Accepted Trades (per commodity Grade),

      • Low=>The Lowest Price of among Executed & Accepted Trades (per commodity Grade),

      • Open=>Price of Opening/first Executed & Accepted Trade (per commodity Grade),

      • Change=> [OpenPrice] - [LastPrice] (i.e Difference between Opening and Last executed price)

      • Volume=> Sum of all the Executed & Accepted Trades Quantity.

e.      ‘Market’ order types will not be displayed under the market watch screen, his just to control market manipulations both by buyers and sellers like if traders know there is a sell order open with a market order type he/she may enter the least price for buying. And in such case when there is Market buy order higher price for selling will be provided by traders of course within the price limit range.

Market Depth

The Market Depth section enables an authorized user to view specific contract’s current market depth (order book), it shows a list of orders displaying both Buy (Yellow part) and Sell (Green Part) side of the market order status. System displays the current Bid (buy) and Ask (sell) offers in terms of quantity and price available for a particular commodity. In fact, this section shows how the matching engine works by arranging the buying and selling orders based on Price-Time Priority algorithm (For buy order the best price will be the higher price given and for Sell order the best price is the least price given whereas the best time is the prior time for both cases this is the algorithm the matching engine uses to match trades). .

  • User can view order book or market depth of a specific commodity under the Market depth being either from Market Watch or My Watch List tabs.

  • If user wants to see the Market Depth of a Specific Commodity Grade he/she can click on either Symbol or Warehouse or Year of a specific commodity grade under the Market Watch/ My Watch List Data Grid Result-Commodity Section then system populates the selected contract's market depth (buying and selling orders of all traders are available at the market depth with their price, time and available volume information) on the Trader's Market Depth Section shown below.

Sell Order Entry

 

This is where an authorized user submits a sell order for commodities he/she is licensed with. Here a sell order or an instruction can be submitted by representatives or clients respectively.

The system do not allow authorized user to submit an order of quantity which has decimal value. Per each order the system does not allow submitting an order more than 20 LOTs in quantity. Per each day the system does not allow authorized user to submit a sum of orders more than 200 LOTs in quantity. For each particular Member or Client the order submission form displays tradable commodities of that particular session.

Every time a sell order is submitted by the user the system deducts the submitted order quantity from the available quantity. And whenever order is rejected it also updates the available quantity with the rejected quantity.

In here a specific authorized Representative can submit an order for his clients and a logged in Client can submit an instruction for the representative which needs approval or directly taken as an order according to the client type STP or Non-STP. [Details shown below]

Four types of Sell Order submission options:

1.      Directly entering the order under the sell order submission form.

2.      From Market Watch screen by clicking on either BidQty or BidPrice value of a specific commodity grade.

3.       From My Watch List screen by clicking on either BidQty or BidPrice value of a specific commodity grade.

4.    From the MCP tab by clicking on specific commodity grade under the Position Summary tab Warehouse Receipts section (Symbol) system fills details under sell order submission form. The information that will be loaded with one click under the sell order submission form is complete including for whom the order is and also the available quantity unlike the other three mentioned above.

 

Note: There is a pre-open session before the beginning of a specific session where users can enter sell or buy order before the actual trade time starts. Here users can bargain prices by entering orders, can edit or cancel GTC or GTD orders but the entered orders will not be executed even if they meant to be until the normal trade time starts.

 

The three types of sell orders:

Limit Order- This order type is a type where the seller specifies the price of the commodity and the order is shown under the market watch section.

Market order – This is a type of order is placed first by clicking the Market checkbox; it is where the seller wants to sell the commodity at any available price at that moment in the market. When the user enters a “market” order he/she doesn’t specify any price and it will match all the opposite buy orders (if there are such orders in the order-book) until the entire quantity is traded. This type of order is not shown under the market watch section.

h.      Limit Price- Price the seller requires to sell his/her commodity (required only for normal order type). The price typed must be within the daily price limits available for that session. k.        Validity – The user can choose for how long the buy order will stay in the Market Depth before execution.

Day- By default this option is selected since it is assumed that the order will be in the system until the end of the current session.

GTC (Good Till Canceled) – If the order is not executed during the current trading session, it will be kept by the system during the next trading sessions until it will be executed or canceled by the user. The same thing happens with the unexecuted quantity in case of partial execution. The maximum period during which a GTC order can be displayed in the Market Depth if the user does not Cancel it, is until the maximum expiry date defined by the exchange. After the validity period expires, the order is removed from the system.

GTD (Good Till Date) – Here the user can select a particular day until which the order can stay in the system or waiting to be executed. When the deadline expires, the order or the unexecuted quantity is removed from the market.  When the user selects GTD option an additional field is displayed “Expiry Date”, so that the expiry date can be selected. Appendix

 

  1. The trade matching rules on the ECX Trading Platform is FIFO which is based on Price and Time which is      for buy order the best price will be the higher price given and for Sell order the best price is the least price given whereas the best time is the prior time for both cases this is the algorithm the matching engine uses to match trades..

  2. A trade is matched when in the system there is a Buy order and a Sell order, and the Buying price is Equal or Higher than the Selling price.

  3. If the Buy order was submitted first, the trade will be concluded at the price of the Buying order.

  4. If the Sell order was submitted first, the trade will be concluded at the price of the Selling order.

  5. If there are several orders that can be matched, the matching will start with the best ranked order in the Market Depth.

  6. The trade quantity will be the minimum between the Buying Quantity and the Selling Quantity. After the trade is executed, the remaining quantity of a partial executed order will keep its position in the Market Depth.

  7. If there are two buy and sell orders with Market order type the system will wait until it gets other order with a limit price then it will pick that price otherwise it will not be executed.

  8. As a rule two clients of a member can perform trade by their representative, which is a ‘Match Trade’; whereas member can’t perform trade with clients under him/her (‘Self trade’) that is member trying to buy from or sell to its client is strictly forbidden and system does not allow.
